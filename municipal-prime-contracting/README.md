# Municipal Prime Contracting: End-to-End Digital Infrastructure Ownership

## The Problem
Municipal clients need one accountable partner for the full scope of a technology
project, rather than a patchwork of vendors with no single owner of the outcome,
technical or otherwise.

## The Solution
Ventured Sole LLC serves as Prime Contractor on municipal technology engagements,
owning the outcome end to end, from initial scope through go-live, rather than
handing off pieces to disconnected vendors.

## What This Involves in Practice

**Vendor and subcontractor management:** coordinating a specialized subcontractor
for site design and development, translating client requirements into technical
scope the subcontractor can execute against, and holding a single point of
accountability to the client throughout.

**Staging and release management:** managing the staging review process across
multiple rounds of revisions, coordinating punch-list cycles between the client
and the subcontractor, and sequencing go-live against real business constraints
(council presentation dates, leadership approval availability).

**Stakeholder communication across technical and non-technical audiences:**
translating technical decisions and tradeoffs for city leadership and elected
officials, while also communicating scope and requirements clearly enough for
a subcontractor's technical team to execute without ambiguity.

**Contract and compliance oversight:** managing a signed Professional Services
Agreement reviewed by outside counsel, ensuring the engagement stays within
scope and any changes are handled through a documented change process rather
than informal requests.

**Direct technical delivery alongside vendor coordination:** for this same
client relationship, also personally architected and deployed [SoleLoop](../soleloop),
a serverless AWS platform built in-house rather than outsourced, demonstrating
that prime contracting here means owning both the vendor-managed and
directly-built portions of a client's technical footprint.
